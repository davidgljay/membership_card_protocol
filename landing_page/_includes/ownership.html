Cards Are Owned by the People and Organizations That Use Them

The Membership Card Protocol is decentralized. Taryn uses an app to manage the cards on her phone and back them up, but she can easily switch between a dozen apps that perform this service whenever she wants to. No one company owns the Membership Card Protocol, which lets Taryn choose the cheapest, most privacy-protecting, and most values-aligned option available. When deciding on a new app, she can examine reviews from experts in security and trust and safety to ensure that it is well designed and thoroughly tested.

Cards can be created for a fraction of a penny, but Taryn decides to pay $10 per month for a service with almost unlimited card creation and more bells and whistles. This money goes to building better software, and to helping keep the card ecosystem safe and secure.
